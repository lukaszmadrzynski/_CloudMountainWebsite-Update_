Yunnan Four
Kingdoms

Kunming, Dali, Lijiang & Shangri-La

The classic Yunnan cultural loop in seven days, from Kunming to Kunming. Walk through four historical kingdoms - Dian of Kunming, Nanzhao of Dali, Mu of Lijiang, and the Tibetan realm of Shangri-La. Ride the high-speed train to Erhai Lake and Shibaoshan's 1,000-year-old grottoes, wander the Tea Horse Road village of Shaxi, lose yourself in Lijiang's UNESCO Old Town, learn the Tibetan art of Tangka painting, trek the Tiger Leaping Gorge, and end at Songzanlin's prayer halls. Three UNESCO sites, four ethnic cultures, one well-paced loop.

What Makes This Tour Special

Tour Highlights

Dian-era lanes, teahouses, lakeside willow light

Kunming Old Town and Green Lake Park

Walk Kunming's old stone lanes and Dian-era teahouses before settling at Green Lake Park, ringed by willow trees and beloved as the city's gathering spot for tai chi and lakeside tea. Welcome dinner at a Yunnan restaurant sets the tone for the four kingdoms ahead.

Bai culture, mountain-and-lake setting

Dali Old Town

Stroll the cobblestone streets of Dali Old Town, the former capital of the Nanzhao Kingdom, rich in Bai ethnic culture. Optional 12-km bike ride around Erhai Lake through rice paddies and villages, or free time to discover hidden cafes, temples, and gardens.

1,000-year-old Buddhist carvings

Shibaoshan Mountain Grottoes

Hike the Mount Shibaoshan - a sacred mountain with 1,000-year-old Buddhist grottoes carved into the cliff face, plus panoramic views over the surrounding valleys. A living historical site, not a museum.
DETAILED SCHEDULE

Tour Itinerary

  • Morning arrival at Kunming Changshui Airport, transfer to your boutique hotel, and a quiet first walk as you settle into the 1,900m plateau - the light here is dry, the air thin and clear. As afternoon slips into golden hour, stroll the willow-lined paths of Green Lake Park - the cultural soul of modern Kunming and the ancient heartland of the Dian Kingdom, Yunnan's first recorded civilization (2nd century BCE). Welcome dinner at a Yunnan restaurant: cross-the-bridge noodles, steam-pot chicken, the kind of slow food that sets the tone for the next seven days.
  • Board the high-speed train at Kunming Station. Two hours of Yunnan rolling past the window - rice paddies, red-earth hills, the Cangshan range drawing closer. Arrive in Dali by lunch, then an afternoon on Erhai Lake's eastern shore - an optional 12-km bike ride through fields, Bai villages, and lakeside lanes, or a slower walk. The lake is bigger than it looks; the light off it is older than the towns. Dinner and a wander through Dali Old Town - the former capital of the Nanzhao Kingdom, all cobblestone alleys, Bai carved-stone facades, and a string of quiet teahouses. Try a local baba pastry with plum wine.
  • Drive into the Jianchuan hills to Mount Shibaoshan - a sacred mountain with 1,000-year-old Buddhist grottoes carved into the cliff face, plus a Bai-shrine complex still in active pilgrimage use. The cliff carvings have weathered the centuries with surprising colour. A slow afternoon in Shaxi - one of the best-preserved Tea Horse Road market towns in Yunnan. Stone bridges over the Heihui River, the centuries-old Xingjiao Temple, alleys still used by horse traders' descendants. The old caravan rest-day feel, without the Lijiang crowds. Late-afternoon drive north through the highland country, watching the light change as you climb toward Lijiang. Check into your Naxi courtyard hotel; a light dinner in the Old Town.
  • A Naxi cultural guide meets you at the hotel and the day unfolds. Begin at the Mu Residence - the former ruling Mu family compound, with its mountain-facing gate and intricate woodwork - a quiet counterpoint to the bustle of the Old Town just outside its walls. Then wander the cobblestone alleys of Lijiang's UNESCO Old Town (Dayan) - the wooden Mu Palace quarter, the lively Zhongyi Market, the cobbled waterfront canals. Stop at the Wenchang Palace for its traditional Naxi architecture and quiet courtyard, and end the afternoon with a short performance of Naxi Ancient Music - a medieval-era form still played by local elder ensembles, recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage. The day closes at the Dongba Cultural Museum with a hands-on workshop: write a few Dongba pictographs - one of the world's few pictographic scripts still in use - and craft a small sheet of traditional Dongba paper, a handmade paper from local shrubs known for its remarkable durability, with surviving examples over 1,000 years old. Take-home: your own pictograph and a piece of paper.
  • Dawn at the Lashi Plateau Wetland Reserve (2,440m) - Common Crane, Black Stork, Bar-headed Goose returning to roost at first light. Then south to the legendary Tiger Leaping Gorge - a 4-km hike between the Jinsha River and the snow range, one of the deepest river canyons on Earth. Drive on toward Shangri-La through increasingly Tibetan country - prayer flags on the slopes, yaks on the road. Late afternoon: a quiet wander through Dukezong Old Town - white-washed Tibetan homes, prayer wheels, the famous 21-metre prayer wheel spinning at the heart of town. Yak-butter tea by the fire.
  • A morning at the magnificent Songzanlin Monastery - the 'Little Potala Palace', the most important Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Yunnan. Active monastery, monks in residence, prayer halls open to visitors. A local Tibetan guide explains the iconography and the daily rhythm. Then a hands-on afternoon with a Songzanlin-affiliated artist learning the basics of Tangka - the intricate Tibetan Buddhist painting tradition. Mineral pigments, gold-line work, and a one-piece take-home. A cultural experience unique to Tibetan Yunnan - not a souvenir shop. All aboard for the fast train back to Kunming - 3-4 hours of highland light fading into the lowland dusk. A quiet meal on board, a final look back at the mountains, the rhythm of the rails.
  • Free time for last wanderings, a final Yunnan coffee, the things you didn't get to on Day 1. Then a private transfer to Kunming Changshui Airport for the flight home. Safe travels - and a piece of Yunnan to take with you.
